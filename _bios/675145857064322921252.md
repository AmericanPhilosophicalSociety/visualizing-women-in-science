biography: Dorothy Wiehl worked in the field of statistics on issues of public health and population. She graduated from the University of Wisconsin where she studied under Wilford I. King, who recommended her to the US Public Health Service. She worked with Edgar Sydenstricker on the health of textile factory workers and pellagra at George Washington University where she received her MA. For 37 years she was the editor of the Milbank Quarterly, a journal on population health and health policy.
