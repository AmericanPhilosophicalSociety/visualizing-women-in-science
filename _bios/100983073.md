biography: Mary Adelaide Nutting was a Canadian nurse and educator. Nutting was part of the inaugural class of the Johns Hopkins school of nursing. After graduating in 1891, she stayed at Johns Hopkins, taking a position as head nurse. She was promoted to superintendent and principal of the nursing school shortly thereafer. In this position she critically refomed the nursing program. Her efforts led to an increase in the academic rigor of nursing programs and greatly improved the quality of nurses' education.
