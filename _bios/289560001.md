biography: Annie Porter was an English zoologist and parasitologist. She studied at the University College London and worked subsequently at the Quick Laboratory in Cambridge. In 1917, she moved to South Africa, working as Head of the Department of Parasitology at the South African institue for Medical Research in Johannesburg. In 1933, she became a research associate in zoology at McGill University in Montreal. She eventually returned to England. In 1928, she was nominated for the Ellen Richards Memorial Prize by the South African Federation for her exceptional work in parasitology.
