biography: Ann Ginsberg is an American molecular biologist who currently serves as deputy director for TB vaccines in the Global Health Division of the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. Ginsberg holds a PhD from Washington University and an MD from Columbia University. She previously worked at the Laboratory of Biochemistry at the National Heart Institute. During this time, she corresponded with Mildred Cohn, asking Cohn for feedback on a paper she wrote with Barbara Joyce. Ginsberg's current work concerns the development of tuberculosis vaccines and treatments for the developing world.
