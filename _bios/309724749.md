biography: Lotte Strauss was a German American pathologist. After studying in Germany and Italy, Strauss came to the United States, where she spent the majority of her career at Mount Sinai Hospital in New York City. Strauss was a pioneer in pediatric pathology, and contributed greatly to the understanding of fetal development pathology. The condition eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis was previously named Churg-Strauss syndrome, honoring her contributions to the understanding of it.
