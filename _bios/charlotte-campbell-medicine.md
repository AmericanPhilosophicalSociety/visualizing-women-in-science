biography: Charlotte Campbell worked in medical mycology, the study of fungi that cause diseases in humans and other organisms. She is known for her work in researching the epidemiology and treatment of histoplasmosis. Campbell served as the medical mycology chief at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research and as an associate professor of medical mycology at Harvard Universities School of Public Health before becoming a full professor. Following her academic retirement, she spent three years working for the American Society for Microbiology as well as volunteered with battered women and at Brigham and Women's Hospital.
