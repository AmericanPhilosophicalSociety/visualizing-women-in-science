biography: Virginia Kneeland Frantz was a pathologist and educator who made discoveries in the study of thyroid, breast, and pancreatic cancers. In 1922, she became the first woman surgery intern at New York Presbyterian Hospital. She then went on to teach surgery at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons. In 1959, Frantz wrote a study on tumors of the pancreas which became a standard text in the field.
