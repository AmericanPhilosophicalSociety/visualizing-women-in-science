biography: Margaret Pittman was an American bacteriologist. Over her decades-long career at the National Institutes of Health, she did groundbreaking work in the study of typhoid, cholera, and pertussis. This work paved the way for the development of vaccines against these diseases. Early in her career, Pittman worked at the Rockefeller Institute with Rufus Ivory Cole and came into contact with several people in this network.
