biography: Virginia Gildersleeve was an American academic known for being a long-term dean of Barnard College. She was co-founder of the International Federation of University Women, and the only woman delegated by the United States to the April 1945 San Francisco United Nations Conference on International Organization, which negotiated the charter for and the creation of the United Nations. Gildersleeve also advocated for women's equality and challenged commonly held beliefs about sexuality and the role of women in society.
