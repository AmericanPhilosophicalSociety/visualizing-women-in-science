biography: Phyllis Q. Edwards was an American medical researcher. She was educated at University of California Los Angeles and received her MD from University of California San Francisco in 1942. She later studied at Harvard, receiving a doctorate in public health in 1958. Focusing on public health and tuberculosis research, Edwards held many positions globally, working in Paris, Copenhagen, Washington DC, Atlanta, and Tucson.
