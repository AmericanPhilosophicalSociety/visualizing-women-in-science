She was a pioneer in the field of toxicology where she studied occupational illnesses and the negative effects of industrial metals and chemical compounds on the human body. She published several studies that helped raise awareness of dangers in the workplace. Later, in 1919, she became the first woman appointed to the faculty at Harvard Medical School, serving in their new Department of Industrial Medicine.
