biography: Natasha Goldowski Renner was a physicist. Born in Moscow, she fled Russia in 1917 for France, where she finished her education and received her PhD from the University of Paris in 1939. She worked in France until the Nazi occupation and fled for the United States. In the United States, she worked on the Manhattan Project, creating an aluminum coating to protect uranium fuel in plutonium production reactors. After the end of the war, she lost her security clearance as part of the Red Scare. She spent the rest of her career as an educator, teaching at Princeton, Black Mountain College, and Alfred College.
