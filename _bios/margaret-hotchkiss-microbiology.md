biography: Microbiologist Margaret Hotchkiss worked on bacteria in seawater and sewage as well as fungi that cause disease. She was a visiting researcher at Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, a bacteriologist at the New Jersey Agricultural Experiment Station, and a distinguished professor at the University of Kentucky. In 1957, she was elected as a fellow of the American Academy of Microbiology. Hotchkiss retired from teaching in 1964 but continued conducting research.
