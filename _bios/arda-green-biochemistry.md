biography: Arda Green worked in biochemistry and became best known for her discovery of the neurotransmitter serotonin as well as the discovery of the chemical recation that causes fireflies to light up at night (bioluminescence). Green also contributed to Gerty Cori and Carl Cori's research on the Cori cycle by showing how pH affects hemoglobin's ability to bind and transport oxygen. The American Chemical Society awarded her the Garvan-Olin medal in 1958.
