biography: Teresina Patiño de Pinzón was a Panamanian tuberculosis researcher. In 1946, she was granted a fellowship by the Panama-United States Cooperative Fellowship Program to conduct research in the United States. She was placed in Florence Seibert's lab, where the two studied tuberculosis together. After finishing her research in the United States, Patiño returned to Panama, where she helped direct a program to vaccinate the population against tuberculosis.
