biography: Hilda Pope Willett was an American bacteriologist and microbiologist. She studied microbiology and biochemistry at Duke University, focusing on tuberculosis reserach. She received her PhD in 1949 and was one of the first women to receive a PhD in microbiology at Duke. Afterwards, she became a professor at Duke, working in the departments of microbiology and immunology. She remained there for the rest of her career.
