biography: Lucy Shapiro's work has advanced the understanding of asymmetric cell differentiation and has assisted the development of novel cures to fight antibiotic resistance and infectious disease. She designed a new class of antimicrobial compounds based on boron. She was awarded the National Medal of Science from President Barack Obama, in addition to being elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the National Academy of Sciences, the National Academy of Medicine, and the American Academy of Microbiology. She served as a White House advisor on bioterrorism threats.
