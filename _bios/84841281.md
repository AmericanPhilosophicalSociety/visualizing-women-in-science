biography: Elizabeth S. ("Tibby") Russell worked in the field of mammalian development. She did significant genetic research on mutations and hemoglobin through her study of mice. She served on the council of the National Academy of Sciences, was a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the Vice-President and later the President-elect of the Genetics Society of America, and was on the advisory council of the National Institute on Aging. She also was a Guggenheim Fellow and was elected to the Maine Women's Hall of Fame.
